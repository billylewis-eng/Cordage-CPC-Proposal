A real project, start to finish.

Jake gets instructed on a new site — change of use from pub to four residential units, New Forest District Council. Here's what the tool does.

CPC Knowledge Base — Claude
Step 1 — Start a new project
Do we have New Forest Local Plan policies in the library?
New Forest District Council: No policies ingested yet.
Jake drops the Local Plan Part 2 PDF into the Dropbox ingestion folder...
New Forest LP2 processed — 47 policies extracted and added to the library.
Step 2 — Draft from past work
The Coach House is a detached two-storey pub with a car park to the rear and beer garden to the south. It's on the High Street within the settlement boundary, not listed, not in a conservation area. Draft me a site description in CPC's tone and style.
The application site comprises a detached two-storey public house known as The Coach House, situated on the High Street within the defined settlement boundary of Ringwood. The property benefits from a car park to the rear and beer garden to the south. The site is not Grade Listed, nor does it fall within a Conservation Area…
Based on: New Inn PS (July 2025), Section 2.0
Step 3 — Policy analysis
What NPPF and New Forest Local Plan policies apply to pub-to-residential change of use?
NPPF: Paragraph 97 (community facilities), Paragraph 11d (tilted balance), Paragraph 135 (design)

New Forest Local Plan: Policy HOU1 (housing), Policy COM1 (community facilities), Policy ENV3 (design and local distinctiveness)

How CPC has cited these before: In the New Inn PS, you argued COM1 was satisfied because the pub had been marketed for 18 months with no viable offers. In the Hounsdown appeal, you used the 5-year supply shortfall (1.92 years) to trigger the tilted balance.
Sources: New Inn PS, Hounsdown Appeal Statement
Step 4 — Summarise consultant reports
Summarise the key findings and tell me which policies they engage.
Drainage (TPA): Site is Flood Zone 1, low surface water risk. No objection subject to SuDS condition. Engages Policy ENV1.

Ecology (Kingdom Ecology): No protected species found. BNG achievable via native planting scheme. Engages Policy ENV2 and NPPF Paragraph 186.
